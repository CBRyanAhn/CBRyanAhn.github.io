My research work predominantly sits at the nexus of construction, human factor, and artificial Intelligence and empowers society and the construction industry to build smart, safe, and connected living and working spaces. Specifically, my core research includes smart and safe construction using IoT and AI, education and training using VR/MR/AR, and human-robot collaboration in construction.

I was an associate professor in Seoul National University (2022-2024), an assistant professor in Texas A&M University (2017-2021) and the University of Nebraska-Lincoln (2012-2017). Currently, I am serving as an executive officer in the ASCE Computing Division Data Sensing and Analysis Committee, and I have served as the president of the Korean-American Construction Engineering and Project Management Association. My specialization lies in smart construction, and my research has been recognized by many awards, including Best Paper Awards in the ASCE International Conference on Computing in Civil Engineering (2019) and the International Conference on Construction Engineering and Project Management (2013).

I earned my B.S. and M.S. degrees from Seoul National University (SNU), and I received my Ph.D. in Civil Engineering from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in 2012.
